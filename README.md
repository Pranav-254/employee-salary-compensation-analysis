# Employee Salaries and Compensation Analysis

This project provides a detailed look into public sector employee compensation, focusing on salary distributions, overtime pay, and longevity pay across various departments. The analysis uses structured data to identify trends in total compensation and workforce demographics.

## Project Overview
The primary goal of this analysis is to evaluate how base salaries and additional pay components (overtime and longevity) are distributed across different organizational divisions and genders.

## Key Insights
* **Departmental Compensation**: Comprehensive tracking of total compensation across major departments such as Alcohol Beverage Services (ABS) and Technology and Enterprise Business Solutions (TBS).
* **Salary Averages**: Calculation of average base salaries by department, with some departments like the Office of Food Systems Resilience showing averages above $112,000.
* **Pay Components**: Analysis of the relationship between base salary and supplemental pay, including significant overtime expenditures in departments like Fire and Rescue Services.
* **Workforce Demographics**: Segmentation of salary data by gender, providing a clear count of the male and female workforce within the dataset.
* **Division-Level Breakdown**: Detailed reporting at the division level, allowing for granular views of infrastructure, cloud services, and administrative divisions.

## Dataset Description
The analysis is based on a dataset containing over 10,000 employee records  with the following attributes:
* **Department & Division**: Organizational identifiers for each employee.
* **Base Salary**: The fundamental annual pay rate.
* **Overtime & Longevity Pay**: Additional compensation earned through extra hours or years of service.
* **Total Compensation**: The sum of all pay components.
* **Gender & Grade**: Demographic and hierarchical classifications.

## Technical Stack
* **Excel/CSV Data Modeling**: Source data management.
* **Pivot Table Analysis**: Summary statistics and departmental aggregations.

## Usage
1. Open the `Employee_Salaries.csv` file to view raw compensation records.
2. Refer to the `Pivot Tables.csv` for summarized data on departmental averages and total pay counts.
